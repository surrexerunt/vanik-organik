organic health

The term “organic” refers to the way agricultural products are grown and processed. While the regulations vary from country to country, in the EU organic crops must be grown without the use of synthetic pesticides, bioengineered genes (GMOs), petroleum-based fertilizers, and sewage sludge-based fertilizers. Organic food labeling guarantees all this. In Europe, there is the “Pan-European Agreement on Organic Production of Agricultural Products” No. 2092/91 dated June 24, 1991 (Council Regulation No 2092/91 of 24 June 1991 on organic production of agricultural products). To get the appropriate certificate, the au pair will have to undergo a very serious test: inspectors check everything from the composition of the soil and its proximity to the roads to processing and packaging methods.
